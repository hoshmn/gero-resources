Geropsych Resource Map
An interactive tool for exploring resources supporting older adults

This interactive map consolidates thousands of community resources gathered by therapists-in-training into a searchable, geographically organized reference tool. It is designed for use by older adults, their families and caregivers, and the clinicians who support them. Resources span categories including elder care, housing support, mental health services, grief counseling, legal aid, and dementia-specific programs.

James Hillman (1999) writes that character in later life is shaped not by adding to oneself but by revealing what was always there — a process requiring conditions of safety, continuity, and support. For clinicians working with older clients, this means that the deep psychological work of aging cannot happen in isolation from the material realities of a person's life. When a client is navigating housing instability, caregiver burnout, or lack of transportation, the therapeutic hour cannot hold everything. As Sheenagh Burns (2008) observed from geropsychology practice in long-term care settings, effective work with older adults requires the clinician to function as a connector to systems of support, not merely an individual practitioner.

Research on aging consistently finds that social connectedness and access to services are among the strongest predictors of wellbeing in later life (Cornwell & Waite, 2009). Yet other research consistently identifies a substantial unmet need for mental health services among older adults, with practical barriers — including not knowing where to go — among the most commonly cited obstacles to care (Lavingia et al., 2020). This tool was built to fill that need.

TECHNICAL NOTE: When resources are added to the spreadsheet, an App Script runs with a geocoder to find the resource location and add lat/lng values based on its name, city, county, and state. Rows missing these details may appear in the wrong location on the map or not at all. The lat/lng values can be manually corrected on the spreadsheet (scroll all the way to the right to find them).
